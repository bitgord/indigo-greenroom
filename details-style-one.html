In Conversation: Ringo Starr

  • By Etye Sarner --
  • 6 Jan 2017 --

On October 20, 2015, world acclaimed musician and author Ringo Starr dropped by Indigo Bay-Bloor in downtown Toronto to chat with CEO and Chief Booklover Heather Riesman about his photographic memoir, Photograph.

The former Beatle spoke of his modest upbringing in England where as a child he discovered his love for music in an unusual setting, a hospital. Ringo, unfortunately, spent a considerable amount of time in a hospital due to Tuberculosis. It was at the hospital where a Nurse would introduce Ringo to various instruments, sparking his love music. Ringo chose drums as his favourite and from that point on decided that he wanted to be a drummer, not a pianist, a guitarist or anything else. However, Ringo and his family could not afford buying drums and so in his interview with Heather Riesman, Ringo speaks of his early struggles to get a hold of drums that he would need to borrow. In the interview, Ringo continues to talk about his time with The Beatles and their early success showcased on the famous Ed Sullivan show in February of 1964.

That 10,000-hour thing, well we put 10,000 in, just in Germany.

Photograph, Ringo’s memoir as appeared in the interview, showcases Ringo’s photographs which include rare and unseen photographs taken by Ringo and other photos from his family albums giving unprecedented insight into his life. Photograph can be picked up online or in store.
